# 11 Free Vendor Wedge Lock

## 1) Why this document exists
This document locks the current free version of the vendor handoff product so future agents do not accidentally turn the free wedge into either:
- a weak teaser that no vendor would actually use
- or a broad pseudo-SaaS that collapses the paid setup and recurring layers

The free version should stay sharp, useful, and intentionally narrow.

## 2) Locked one-line definition
The free version of BackflowPath lets a small busy vendor office turn an annual notice or failed test into a customer-ready brief in about two minutes, send it as a link or PDF, and recover qualified customer attention through BackflowPath proof rails.

## 3) What the free version must prove
The free wedge is not here to prove broad software adoption.

It is here to prove four things:
1. a vendor office can create one real sendable artifact fast
2. a customer can understand why they were contacted and what to do next
3. the artifact is strong enough that a vendor would actually send it
4. the artifact can still recover attention back into BackflowPath without weakening trust

If the free version does not prove those four things, there is no credible paid layer above it.

## 4) Target user for the free version
The practical first free user is:
- a small busy backflow-related office
- usually owner plus admin or coordinator
- handling annual notices, failed tests, or similar repeated explanation work

The free wedge is not optimized first for:
- large teams with complex permissions
- utility staff
- deeply integrated field-service organizations

## 5) Free version promise
The promise of free is:

"You can send one good customer explanation today."

It is not:
- "you can run your whole office here"
- "you can replace your compliance system here"
- "you can replace your CRM here"

## 6) What free must include
Free must remain good enough for one real job.

That means it must include:
- a customer-ready brief
- a sendable link
- a sendable PDF
- the local utility or issue context
- official proof paths such as the full rule or official program
- a recognizable vendor sender line
- customer-facing next-step clarity
- BackflowPath recovery rails that let the customer click into trusted local context

Free should remain usable for:
- annual notice follow-up
- failed test follow-up

## 7) What free must not try to solve yet
Free should not drift into full office memory or multi-user workflow.

Do not treat these as part of the locked free wedge:
- server-side company workspaces
- team permissions
- billing and subscription management
- broad account systems
- generalized CRM behavior
- official submission system replacement
- fully white-labeled output
- broad workflow automation

If those appear, they should be treated as later paid layers, not as free-surface requirements.

## 8) Free version trust rules
The free wedge only works if the trust position is strong.

So free must keep these rules:
- public and internal surfaces must stay clearly separated
- internal notes must never leak into the public brief
- the customer brief must keep one-click access to local rule proof
- the free output must not imply that BackflowPath sends email or SMS for the vendor
- the artifact must not feel like a fake teaser or crippleware

## 9) Free version branding rules
The free wedge should currently stay vendor-first and co-branded.

Why:
- the vendor needs the output to feel usable with its own customer relationship
- BackflowPath still needs the customer recovery loop
- official proof and trust should remain visible

That means the preferred free output structure is:
- vendor visible first
- BackflowPath visible as proof, rule, or recovery layer

The free wedge should not default to full white-label.

## 10) What free is allowed to do for repeat convenience
Free can include light convenience if it does not become a real paid memory system.

Allowed:
- local browser convenience
- local same-browser "last job" help if it stays tightly scoped
- quick intake helpers from pasted notices or failed results

Not yet part of the locked free definition:
- office-wide saved presets across devices
- durable server-side brief history
- vendor account memory

## 11) Relationship to paid setup
Paid setup exists because free is already good enough to send.

Setup should align the free workflow to one vendor office by adding:
- sender defaults
- annual notice wording defaults
- failed test wording defaults
- PDF wording alignment
- sample briefs for common utility situations

Free therefore should not be weakened just to create a fake setup need.
The right setup need is alignment and convenience, not artificial withholding.

## 12) Relationship to paid recurring
Recurring should not be required for the free wedge to feel complete.

Recurring only becomes valid after repeated office use proves that the vendor wants:
- memory
- reuse
- regeneration
- history
- office continuity

Free proves one sendable success.
Recurring should eventually remove repeated office friction.

## 13) Success signals for the locked free wedge
The free wedge is working when:
- vendors create briefs without needing a homepage pitch
- customers can understand why they were contacted
- the brief gets sent in real jobs
- the same utility and issue combinations repeat
- customer clicks recover into BackflowPath rule or proof surfaces

## 14) Failure signals
The free wedge is not locked if:
- the output feels too weak to send
- the customer still does not know what to do next
- the free surface starts implying full SaaS capability it does not have
- the free layer loses the recovery loop through over-white-labeling
- the free layer becomes so complete on repeat-memory features that setup and recurring no longer have a clean reason to exist

## 15) Decision rules for future agents
- Keep free strong enough for one real customer send.
- Do not intentionally cripple the free brief to force upgrades.
- Do not add broad workspace behavior into free without revisiting the monetization ladder.
- Keep vendor-first co-branding unless the product strategy explicitly changes.
- Treat annual notice and failed test as the default free wedge until real usage proves a broader surface is justified.
