# 01 Query And User Map

## 1) Core jobs to be done
- Tell me if testing is required for my property here.
- Tell me when the test is due and what happens if I ignore it.
- Tell me what a failed test means and what the next repair/retest workflow looks like.
- Tell me the realistic local cost and who can handle it.
- Give me the exact local form, portal, rule, or approved tester list.

## 2) Priority user segments
### Highest-intent users
- Property owners who received an annual testing notice.
- Managers dealing with a failed device test.
- Owners installing irrigation, fire line, or new assemblies that trigger compliance.

### Secondary users
- Buyers checking whether a commercial or HOA property carries a compliance burden.
- Contractors who need a quick public explainer page to send to clients.

## 3) Query families by funnel stage
### "Need to know if this applies"
- `{city} backflow test`
- `{utility} backflow requirement`
- `who needs a backflow preventer`
- `irrigation backflow requirement {city}`

### "Need to act now"
- `annual backflow testing {city}`
- `submit backflow report {utility}`
- `approved backflow tester {city}`
- `failed backflow test next steps`

### "Need a budget"
- `backflow test cost {city}`
- `rpz repair cost`
- `backflow repair and retest cost`

### "Need a provider"
- `backflow tester near me`
- `certified backflow tester {city}`
- `backflow repair company {city}`

## 4) Canonical SEO unit selection
- Default canonical entity: utility or water authority.
- City pages exist only when users search by city but compliance is utility-owned.
- Metro aggregation pages are for tester discovery, not for the core rules.

## 5) Priority page set
### Evergreen pages
- What is backflow testing
- Who needs a backflow preventer
- Backflow test cost
- Failed test next steps
- Device type guide: RPZ vs DCVA vs PVB

### Local utility pages
- Utility home rule page
- Annual testing
- Failed test
- Approved testers
- Irrigation
- Fire line

## 6) Search positioning rules
- Do not chase national "what is backflow" head terms as the main growth path.
- Use local compliance pages to win long-tail and let evergreen guides support internal links.
- Favor pages where the user needs a form, portal, list, due date, or consequence.

## 7) AEO targets
- "Do I need annual backflow testing in {city}?"
- "What happens if a backflow device fails?"
- "How much does backflow testing cost in {city}?"
- "Who can submit a backflow test report in {utility service area}?"

## 8) What to avoid
- Generic plumbing lifestyle content.
- Thin city pages with no governing-utility mapping.
- Broad device spec pages without local action intent.
