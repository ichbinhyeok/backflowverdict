# 04 Commercial Model

## 1) Revenue thesis
Backflow is strongest as a direct local sponsor business, not a pure affiliate play.

## 2) Primary monetization paths
- Sponsored tester placements by utility or metro
- Pay-per-call for emergency repair or failed-test fix
- Pay-per-form for annual testing requests
- Premium provider profiles

## 3) Secondary monetization paths
- Compliance reminder subscriptions for property managers
- White-labeled annual reminder service later

## 4) Offer design
### Starter sponsor
- One metro or utility cluster
- featured CTA on approved-testers or failed-test pages

### Premium sponsor
- featured profile
- call tracking
- emergency repair CTA
- category presence across testing + failed-test pages

## 5) CTA logic
- Local rules page -> official source first, sponsor second
- Official approved-testers page -> official list first, sponsored providers in a separate labeled block below it
- Find-a-tester page -> sponsor CTA may be prominent because the page is explicitly non-official
- Cost page -> sponsor CTA above fold
- Failed-test page -> repair CTA stronger than annual testing CTA
- Provider pages -> call, quote, and service area clarity

## 6) Lead handling rules
- No multi-buyer lead resell without explicit user consent.
- If call tracking is used, clearly disclose sponsored placement.
- Never label a sponsor as approved, certified, or authority-listed without source support.
- Keep forms short: name, phone, city, utility, property type, issue type.
- Store source page and page intent for every lead.

## 7) Early sales motion
- Start sponsor outreach after the first pilot utilities are trustworthy.
- Sell on "we explain your exact local rule and route the user when they are ready."
- Avoid selling broad traffic. Sell compliance-intent traffic.
